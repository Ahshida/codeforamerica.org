How it all began

In February of 2013 Code for America selected three partner organizations that were interested in exploring the possibility of adapting Code for America programs to their own local context in three very different locations (the Caribbean, Mexico City and Germany). Code for America worked closely with these partners (Laboratorio para la Ciudad (LabPLC), Mexico City’s office of civic innovation, the Open Knowledge Foundation’s Germany chapter and Slashroots, a civic tech non profit based in Jamaica), to develop their fellowship and Brigade programs and learn from the process of adapting the CfA model to different countries. Those 3 partners, jointly with CfA, came to make up the founding members of the Code for All community.
