Our Approach


Code for America encourages new strategies, including cross-agency collaboration among law enforcement, health and human services departments, schools systems, and others, to implement evidence-based strategies to keep individuals from cycling in and out of the justice system. With particular attention to those communities most impacted by crime and violence, our work is focused on increasing public safety, strengthening public trust, and saving governments money.

This is an emerging area of Code for America’s work. In the past few years, Code for America has worked with Atlanta and Louisville on key issues in their court and jail systems. In Atlanta, we helped improve court efficiency and, via text messaging updates, helped get busy citizens to their court date and avoid jail. In Louisville, Code for America built a real-time dashboard so prosecutors, administrators, and police officers could see the population numbers in the jail and other vital statistics to better inform release, sentencing, and placement decisions.

As part of the 2016 Fellowship, Code for America is partnering with Indianapolis and Vallejo on better integrating data into public safety efforts and strengthening law enforcement relations with the community.

How we work


Code for America’s focus areas bring together work across all of our programs, delivering an impact greater than what each program might achieve on its own. Across our programs, we undertake deep research into the delivery of critical services, develop new digital approaches to health, incubate digital products as they mature, and work with the highest levels of government support to scale digital services. Throughout these efforts, we work to empower our partner organizations through the Principles of 21st Century Government.

Original Research and Digital Work

Fellowship teams work with cities and counties to develop innovative new approaches to key policy goals.

Building and scaling Digital Services

As fellowship teams move on, we work to transform promising new technologies into the mature digital services citizens deserve in the 21st century.

Helping Governments Deliver Digital Services

We document what we learn to make building digital government easy to understand and copy through our library and Peer Network.


Original digital services produced with governments

The Code for America Fellowship Program sends teams of technologists into local governments across the country to work full-time for a year in partnership with government officials and the community. The fellows apply 21st Century Government Principles to important problems, focused on health, economic development, and safety and justice. While the product of the Fellowship is typically an early stage application that improves the delivery of a government service, the process acts as a vehicle for driving cultural and structural change inside of government.


Building and scaling digital services

Beyond the fellowship, our staff technologists, designers, and researchers experiment and invest over time in high potential digital services.

In 2015, we are conducting ongoing research and product development to both improve the user experience and enrollment rate of the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) rate in California, building off foundational work done by the San Francisco Fellowship team in 2013.

You can follow all of our work on Github, and track our performance against key product indicators on our public metrics page.

Our Projects

  • Balance

    Balance allows people who receive food stamps to check their balance via SMS. Balance brings a little dignity and convenience to the business of managing benefits.

    Learn more

  • Clean

    Clean is a simple and dignified online application for CalFresh. We believe that with a good user experience, thoughtful product marketing and the right partnerships that Clean will be able to meaningfully increase the number of people eligible for CalFresh enrolled in the program.

    Learn more

  • Connect

    Connect helps social service clients and enrollment assisters avoid wasting time on hold. Connect allows users to initiate a call to a social services agency via SMS, and get a call back when a human is on the other line.

    Learn more

  • EBT Near Me

    EBT Near Me helps social service clients find important places they can use their EBT card. On their phone or when first enrolled, people can find ATMs without fees and CalFresh retail locations.

    Learn more

  • Promptly

    Promptly text messages San Francisco Human Services Agency clients with important information to keep them enrolled in services.

    Learn more

  • AddressIQ

    In 2013, 52 percent of emergency medical calls in Long Beach, CA came from the same 10 percent of addresses. This dashboard analyzes data in order to identify these "super-utilizers" and help find find ways to coordinate their care, reducing costs and improving outcomes.

    Learn More

  • Open Referral

    People can't take advantage of services they don't know exist. Open Referral is an open data standard that makes it easier to find and share this information.

    Learn More
