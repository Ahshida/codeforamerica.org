Starting in 2015, we are focusing our iterative, user-centered, and data-driven approach to government primarily in three areas: health, economic development, and safety & justice.

Health & Human Services

Health and Dignity by Default

Building technology with empathy that puts the user first can promote a culture of health and better feed, shelter, and treat those in need. Code for America partners with local governments, service organizations, and foundations to apply human centered design and modern technology to dramatically reduce the friction of accessing these critical services.

  • Promptly

    Promptly text messages San Francisco Human Services Agency clients with important information to keep them enrolled in services. First used to keep people enrolled in CalFresh, the app is also a flexible tool to help schedule, monitor, and send text message notifications for any reason.

Safety & Justice

Fair, Safe, and Just

Getting the right information to the right person at the right time -- in the right way -- makes our communities safer and more just. Code for America partners with local governments, foundations, and others to make our public safety and criminal justice systems work for the people, by the people, in the 21st century.

  • CourtBot

    Instead of traveling to the courthouse and standing in line — sometimes for hours — the 200,000 Atlanta residents who receive traffic tickets each year can now text their citation number to get information on how to pay the ticket or get information and reminders about their court dates.

Economic Development

Economic Opportunity for Everyone

Redesigning how workers, entrepreneurs, and businesses interact with local government can foster the kind of growth a whole community can participate in. Code for America partners with local government to develop tools and approaches for stimulating local economic vitality for everyone.

  • OpenCounter

    OpenCounter helps entrepreneurs navigate the business registration process. It asks the right questions, in the right order, so applicants can see the requirements, fees, and processing time that apply for their planned use and location. By making information available across departments, OpenCounter helps municipalities coordinate internal workflows, and build better relationships with new business owners.

  • BizFriendly.

    BizFriend.ly is a digital literacy resource where small to mid-sized businesses can build the skills and understanding to get their company online. Lessons provide entrepreneurs with an easy-to-follow, real-time, step-by-step guide to various programs and web-based services.

  • Infrastructure Map

    With an almost $1 billion backlog of infrastructure debt, the City of Atlanta has pledged to begin improving roads, sidewalks, bridges, and more. The interactive map lets residents, real estate developers, and prospective business owners residents track where the money is going - and even start a conversation with their community about priorities.
