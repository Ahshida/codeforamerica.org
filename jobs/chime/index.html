Chime Jobs

Code for America is a start-up non-profit. One of our projects is Chime CMS, a hosted content management platform built from the ground up for the needs of local governments and their residents. Chime's currently open jobs are below. (For other CfA jobs, see our main jobs page.

Code for America is committed to creating a diverse environment.

Engineer

Code for America is changing the way governments and citizens interact. We need your help.

City websites will become the primary way residents learn about and interact with their local governments. Our Digital Front Door project is working with cities around the country to make those websites radically better. Part of that is building Chime CMS, an innovative content management and hosting platform that makes it easy for city employees to communicate in ways that work for everyone.

We need an experienced engineer to help build Chime and, as we grow, mentor future engineers. Your typical week will include quite a bit of designing, coding, and releasing new features. There will be friendly, free-flowing discussions with the rest of your cross-functional team. As we get new clients, you may spin up and test new virtual servers using our automated infrastructure tools. When production errors happen (which we aim to make rare), you will sometimes jump on one, run it down, and check in a fix (where it will be quickly pushed live by our continuous deployment pipeline). Sound like a lot? Don’t worry, we’re big on collaboration, knowledge sharing, and cross-training. And we end every week with a retrospective, so if something’s not working for us, we’ll fix it.

  • Willing and legally able to work in our San Francisco office

Join us!

Interested? We tried to make applying quick and easy. If you're viewing this on a website with an application form, feel free to fill it out. Or, even better, just send the following in email to jobs-chime@codeforamerica.org:

  1. A link to (or a copy of) something resume-like (e.g., your website, LinkedIn)
  2. Other links you’d like us to see (e.g., blog, GitHub, a project of yours)
  3. To demonstrate that you’ve read this and are a serious applicant, a short answer to just one of these:​
    • What was the last good book, article, or presentation you saw on software development? What made it stand out for you?
    • What’s a favorite open-source library? What do you like about it?
    • What’s the language you learned most recently but haven’t yet had a chance to use in production? What have you learned from it so far?
    • What technology did you most recently mentor a colleague on? How did it go?
    • What are three things you’d change about the language you’ve done most of your recent coding in?

Due to resume spammers and other unneighborly people, we regrettably can't accept applications that don’t include the 3 items listed above. It’s not necessary, but feel free to write more if there’s something you’re eager to say.

We also welcome your questions. We like inquisitive people.
