What to expect from the webinar

Before and After

Before each webinar, each course participant will receive an e-mail summary of what each participant can expect from the webinar, and what they should have prepared. After class, notes will be distributed so that you may share the content with your colleagues, and a recording of the webinar will be available in the Code for America library. However, given the brief duration of the course, it is mandatory that at least one staffperson from your agency be present for each webinar.


During the Webinar

The webinar will be broadcast via a Google Hangout, and facilitated by Garret Jacobs, Peer Network Coordinator at Code for America. Jereme Monteau will be present to introduce himself, and describe the technical support you can expect during the three-week course peperiod.

Coursework for Week 2

  • Identify and Collect Your Data

    • Trail segment data (polylines)
    • Trailhead data (points)
    • Areas data (polygons)

  • Use the OpenTrails Converter and Validator Tools to produce OpenTrails

    • Convert your existing GIS shapefiles
    • Document missing files or attributes
    • Summarize your experience, and how far off your current data is from conformance with Open Trails.
    • Share your data with other agencies taking the OpenTrails Course.

  • Begin working to secure internal buy-in.

    • Identify colleagues that need to be involved in sustainable open data
    • Develope rough estimates of time and resources required.
    • Document anticipated challenges.
