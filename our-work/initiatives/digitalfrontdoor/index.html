Digital Front Door

Cities must meet their citizens where they are spending more and more time: online. Municipal websites are the digital front door to a city- a place for sharing information, conducting business, and collecting feedback about what residents want and need. Join us in our efforts to rethink government websites and improve the experience for both citizens and city employees.

What We're Doing

Helping Cities Use Web Analytics

We're working with cities around the country to deploy our free, open source City Analytics Dashboard to bring staff closer to their online constituents in the "virtual city hall" and to create an opportunity for direct feedback on the work they do online.

Learn More Deploy the app

Partnering on Website Redesigns

We're partnering with the City of Oakland on a website redesign project that will help them understand their residents’ online user needs, create an alpha site, and help them navigate procurement issues to hire a local web development team.

Follow Along Read the Phase 1 Report

Connecting People and Information

We're launching a Digital Front Door community of practice through our Peer Network, an online discussion forum for local government staff to discuss their own website redesign initiatives and share best practices.

Join the Peer Network

Resources for Digital Front Door

We're publishing a set of resources for cities doing website redesigns.

  • Where do I start?

    Use this guide if you are looking for advice on how to plan a new city website alpha, a redesign or overhaul and good tasks to start with.

  • What should a city website do?

    Use this guide to learn about general principles for city websites, how to start publishing effective web content, and how to prioritize transactional needs on your site.

  • Tool: City Analytics Dashboard

    Deploy our City Analytics Dashboard, a free open source tool that uses Google Analytics to help city staff better understand their website’s live traffic.

  • Tool: Resident Survey

    About to start a website redesign? Use this survey to assess the devices and types of internet access used in different parts of your city, evaluate user needs to prioritize content development, and understand where your city has strong and weak connections to users of its website.
