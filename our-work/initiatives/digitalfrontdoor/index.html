DFD Alpha Program - A Redesign Bootcamp for Government Communicators.

The practice and skills needed to approach and execute your city’s website redesign.

Apply Now

Expert-led training by Code for America Staff/Tech Team

Code for America is helping cities to meet their citizens where they are spending more and more time: online. An entire website redesign can be a daunting proposition, often taking years to complete. We have created a workshop series to help you with the practices and skills you need to define the goals of a city redesign as well as how to get started with an alpha to both prove out a better way to build city websites.

What is a DFD Alpha?

A DFD Alpha is a way to address a resident need with a new approach. It allows you to reduce risk, try new ideas and develop better practices around using your website for core city needs.

The best way to prepare for your city-wide website redesign is to create an alpha that lets you try out some ideas and get feedback early.

What you’ll get:

  • A live alpha website that models a new approach to resident needs
  • Weekly workshops and hands-on support from CfA staff
  • A network of practitioners
  • Actionable steps for week-on-week progress

What you commit:

  • 1 hour live remote workshop attendance per week for 10 weeks
  • Around 2 hours of implementation work per week with Alpha department colleagues

More about the Redesign Bootcamp

  • Example workshop sessions:

    • Baselining and analytics
    • Writing for the web
    • Project scoping, user needs definition
    • User testing and recruiting
    • Design values and briefs
    • Digital first publishing and workflows

  • Example user needs:

    How we’re using DfD alphas to improve service delivery

    • Parking information: “How do I pay a parking ticket?”
    • Parks and Recreation programming: “When is the next lap swim?”
    • Legislative participation: “When is the next council meeting?”
    • Job seekers. “What jobs are offered by city hall”
    • Special events. “How do I plan a public event?
    • Trash pick up. “When is trash pick-up for my street?”
    • Report service requests. “My pothole needs filling”

  • How we’ll measure improvement:

    • User experience testing: people are finding what they want to do faster.

Tools

    Deploy our City Analytics Dashboard, a free open source tool that uses Google Analytics to help city staff better understand their website’s live traffic.

    About to start a website redesign? Use this survey to assess the devices and types of internet access used in different parts of your city, evaluate user needs to prioritize content development, and understand where your city has strong and weak connections to users of its website.
