Health and social services delivered with dignity, by default.

Code for America partners with local governments to build and grow digital health and human services focused on users, helping governments transform themselves along the way.

Our Approach

The health focus area works to improve the health of people and the communities they live in. Health depends on many factors: from the environment people live in, to how easy it is to sign up for, use, and deliver digital services.

We work with the wide variety of teams that contribute to this outcome—including city health departments, public health agencies, state offices, and non-profit organizations. Together, we develop digital services to meaningfully improve health outcomes. In the process, we help these teams retool their ways of delivering services to operate more effectively in the digital world.

Get Involved

Pursuing better health in a community often requires partnerships that span the public, non-profit, and private sectors. How we work

Code for America’s focus areas support and bring together work across all of our programs to deliver an impact greater than what an individual program might achieve on its own. Across our programs, we undertake deep research into the delivery of critical services, develop new digital approaches to health, incubate digital products as they mature, and work with the highest level of government support to scale digital services. Throughout these efforts, we work to empower our partner organizations through the Principles of 21st Century Government.

Partnering with governments to produce original digital services

Health Fellowships

The Code for America Fellowship Program recruits teams of technologists for a year of deep partnership with a local government and the community around a new or improved digital service. The output includes an “alpha” product for that digital service, an understanding of the problem space in one community informed by our Peer Network, a local government partner with greater capacity to demonstrate the Principles for 21st Century Government, and strong relationships across the service delivery team that can be leveraged in the future.

Building and scaling digital services

Beyond the fellowship, our staff technologists, designers, and researchers experiment and invest over time in high potential digital services.

In 2015, we are conducting ongoing research and product development to both improve the experience and enrollment rate of the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) rate in California, building off foundational work done by the San Francisco Fellowship team in 2013.

You can follow all of our work on Github, and track our performance against key product indicators on our public metrics page.

Ongoing Work
  • Balance

    Balance allows people who receive food stamps to check their balance via SMS. Balance brings a little dignity and convenience to the business of managing benefits.

  • Clean

    Clean is a simple and dignified online application for CalFresh. We believe that with a good user experience, thoughtful product marketing and the right partnerships that Clean will be able to meaningfully increase the number of people eligible for CalFresh enrolled in the program.

  • Connect

    Connect helps social service clients and enrollment assisters avoid wasting time on hold. Connect allows users to initiate a call to a social services agency via SMS, and get a call back when a human is on the other line.

  • EBT Near Me

    EBT Near Me helps social service clients find important places they can use their EBT card. On their phone or when first enrolled, people can find ATMs without fees and CalFresh retail locations.
