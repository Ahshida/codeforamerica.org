Join us at the fourth annual Code for America Summit

September 23 – 25, 2014

The Yerba Buena Center
San Francisco, CA

The Code for America Summit is an annual gathering of civic technology leaders — ranging from Mayors to entrepreneurs, from community organizers to CTOs — who hold a shared belief that together we can create a government by the people, for the people that works for us all in the 21st century.

More than 80 cities were represented at last year’s event. This year's Summit will bring together even more cities and civic-minded citizens to exchange ideas, explore new ways to solve problems, and identify best practices for improving the way government works. If you are interested in giving a talk or proposing a breakout session, submit your idea here.

The 2014 Summit takes place during Rosh Hashanah. Unfortunately we were unable to avoid this as competition for the limited number of venues in San Francisco only permitted these dates. We recognize that this is a conflict for members of our community, and apologize. We will be offering livestreaming of the keynotes and main-stage content in hopes that all are able to join the event either in-person or online.

Who participates?

Government

The Summit is one of the few opportunities for civic leaders from all levels of government, from cities and counties big and small, to come together and discuss their vision, opportunities, and challenges.

Technologists

Developers, designers, researchers, product managers, data enthusiasts, and technologists of all varieties join the Summit to share stories, ideas and code. These active citizens are turning passion into action and impact.

Network

Transformation doesn’t happen without engagement in a larger ecosystem. The startups, entrepreneurs, foundations, civic hackers, and tech companies who sustain our communities also participate in the dialogue.
