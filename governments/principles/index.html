Principles of 21st Century Government

Over the past five years of building digital services with local governments around the country, we’ve identified several principles that, when in place, help government work for the people, by the people, in the 21st century.

1. Start with people’s needs

In order to work for the people, governments must understand the needs of the people they are serving. The needs of people — not the needs of government — should drive goals that inform all technical and design decisions.

2. Ensure everyone can participate

In order to work by the people, governments need to create ways for every community member to take part in decisions and issues that affect them.

3. Take an iterative approach

Governments that start small and take an iterative approach to problem solving, reduce the risk of failure and get working tools into people’s hands quickly, then make continuous improvements based on feedback.

4. Use data for decision-making

Governments that embrace data-driven approaches, like analytics, performance management, and predictive modeling, can make informed ongoing improvements based on what they are learning.

5. Default to open

When governments collaborate in the open and publish data online, the community can get involved in making Government work better.

6. Know when and how to buy vs. build

Governments that are able to make informed decisions about when to buy and when to build software, avoid vendor lock-in and allow for better project management.

7. Create multidisciplinary teams

Governments that invest in staff to build skills, hire new talent where it’s needed, and build multidisciplinary teams focused on delivery are seeing results.
