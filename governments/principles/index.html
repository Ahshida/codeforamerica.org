Over the past five years of building digital services with local governments around the country, we’ve identified several principles that, when in place and applied to important problems, help government work for the people, by the people, in the 21st century.

1. Design for people’s needs

In order to work for the people, governments must understand the needs of the people they are serving. The needs of people — not the needs of government — should inform technical and design decisions.

Saving time and opening access to Pre-K

The Rhode Island Department of Education partnered with Code for America to redesign the pre-Kindergarten application process. By starting with research and interviews to understand the needs of parents and school administrators, they created a new user-friendly registration tool called Golden Ticket that saved over 800 hours of parent and staff time and led to a 20% increase in registrations. Golden Ticket helped Rhode Island win $19 million in federal funding to expand from 17 classrooms to 60 between 2015 and 2019, giving more kids a great start in life. Read more
