21st Century Government

We belive the government can work, for the people, by the people, in the 21st Century. To do that, governments need to be digital first, policy and delivery need to work toether, and open.

1. Digital first

Digital is fundamentally changing the way governments work and deliver services to the public. 21st Century Governments are using digital technologies to:

  • run their operations
  • make public information easy to find and use
  • deliver quick, easy, and cost effective services

Digital is no longer something that governments can just explore doing, or outsource to a vendor. Today, digital must be a core competency of every government organization. Governments need to invest in people who value the role of digital at all levels of the organization, and know how to evalute and choose the right technology for the job.

2. Policy and delivery work together

Policy and delivery specialists have to work together in a new way.

When policies aren’t delivered well, they’re hard or impossible to use. When policies are hard or impossible to use, it’s as if those policies don’t even exist. Understanding and caring about the nuts and bolts of how services are delivered is just as important as deciding what to deliver.

When the people who decide policy and the people with digital delivery skills work together, our governments can:

  • Focus on user needs and set policy to meet those needs
  • Design and deliver services to meet user needs
  • Keep updating policy and delivery to meet needs better

3. An open platform for everyone makes government better for everyone

We must build a platform on the internet, open to everyone, to shape and take part in delivering government together.

The open platform of the internet has shown us the incredible things we can do when we share with each other and work together.

An open government platform is the latest step in building a more perfect union together. An open platform of government of the people, by the people, for the people through sharing and collaboration.

An open government platform on the internet means:

  • sharing code, ideas, designs, intentions and failures so we can learn from each other
  • sharing information, so everyone can understand what’s working - and what’s not
  • building resources like APIs and using open standards, so it’s easy for others to build and create
  • using technology to make it as easy to be a crowd of hands as a crowd of voices
