Focus on Safety & Justice

In communities across the country, there is a broad consensus that our justice system is not working in a safe, fair and effective way. Prison and jail populations have exploded, recidivism rates are stubbornly high and police/community relations are strained. While overall crime rates are low, certain individuals continue to experience high levels of crime and violence, and too often their needs for safer communities and support to recover go unserved.

To meet these challenges, justice officials need access to accurate, integrated, real-time data to make safe, informed decisions at both the case and system-level. They need digital tools to improve the process of connecting people to much needed health, housing and employment and educational resources, supporting sufficient scale to meet the need. Finally, they need the ability to increase transparency around the inner workings of the justice system, increasing accountability and making possible more productive public dialogue about these pressing issues.

Our Goals

  1. Make criminal justice data, and other relevant data, better integrated, accessible, and accurate to improve decision making at the individual and system-wide level.
  2. Connect the right people to the right interventions to improve public safety outcomes and save money.
  3. Improve community safety and strengthen the relationship between law enforcement and residents, especially in those communities most impacted by crime and violence.

Given the size of these figures (13M jail admissions, $9B to house defendants who don't make bail)...It is troubling that we are not applying data analysis to one of the greatest challenges we face as a society.

ANNE MILGRAM, VICE PRESIDENT FOR CRIMINAL JUSTICE, ARNOLD FOUNDATION

Partnering with governments to produce original digital services

The Code for America Fellowship Program sends teams of technologists into local governments across the country to work full-time for a year in partnership with government officials and the community. The fellows apply 21st Century Government Principles to important problems, focused on health, economic development, and safety and justice. While the product of the Fellowship is typically an early stage application that improves the delivery of a government service, the process acts as a vehicle for driving cultural and structural change inside of government.

Our Projects

  • Courtbot

    Courtbot is a simple web service for handling court case data. It enables city residents who receive traffic tickets to text their citation number to get information on how to pay the ticket or get information and reminders about their court dates.

    Learn more

  • Louisville Jail Dashboard

    Jail Population Management Dashboard helps judges, district attorneys and other stakeholders understand the conditions in the metro jail and use this data to visualize how their decisions affect program, facility and inmate outcomes.

    Learn more

  • AddressIQ

    AddressIQ is a web application that analyzes city data to help identify addresses with a high number of 911 calls and support finding more cost-effective ways to provide those individuals who are high utilizers of emergency services with care and resources.

    Learn more

  • Criminal Case Search

    Criminal Case Search, a web application that enables staff to run customized real-time searches of criminal case information such as defendant demographics, criminal history, criminal charge, and court scheduling information to determine eligibility for a range of alternatives to incarceration.

    Learn more
