RVA Community Partners in Richmond, Virginia

More than one in four Richmond residents live in poverty. Many of those residents have minimal or no access to basic healthcare, causing illness and disease, that if left untreated, puts unnecessary burdens on the city's emergency rooms and hospitals. The fellows are addressing an existing high barrier to entry into many primary care clinics by developing web applications that allow patients to apply for safety-net health services online while also helping organizations share this information as a means to break down these barriers to services.

On behalf of leaders across the Richmond region, we are thrilled to be working with CfA to integrate technology and innovation into our approach to improve healthcare access and utilization by our vulnerable populations. We have an unprecedented opportunity to bring together healthcare, philanthropy, the technology sector and government to engage our community residents in the design of a solution that will fundamentally change the way we interact with the healthcare system.”

Danny Avula, Deputy Director of the Richmond City Health Department
