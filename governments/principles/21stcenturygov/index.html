21st Century Government

We belive the government can work, for the people, by the people, in the 21st Century. To do that, governments need to be online, adaptive, and open.

1. Meet people where they are: online

Government programs and services should be designed to meet people where they are, which in the 21st century, is online.

With more than 80% of American adults online, and a new generation of people coming of age on the internet, governments need to adapt their programs and services to be responsive in the digital age. Major businesses and industries are now running their companies entirely on online software, giving them access to data and insights that improve the operations of their business and improve their bottom line. They are making information easy to find, and delivering their services online, allowing users to access them at anytime of the day or night and on any device.

Digital transformation can not be realized through outsourcing to a vendor or by filing an IT help ticket, digital needs to be a core competency, with skills embedded at all levels of the organization, and owned by those responsible for delivering programs and services to the public.

2. Ensure policy and delivery are adaptive and working together to meet people’s needs

If we can’t implement the policies we create, we can’t govern.

In order for governments to be able to respond swiftly and dynamically to the needs of their community, a new approach to policy making needs to emerge.

  • Policy and delivery need to be intrinsically connected.
  • The needs of users should drive policy decisions.
  • Policies should be updated regularly to respond to user feedback.

The best articulation of this transformation remains the blog post The Strategy is Delivery by Mike Bracken (the UK’s Cabinet Office Executive Director of Digital and the head of the Government Digital Service), which compares the old process for approaching the creation of digital services to the approach the Government Digital Service has taken.

3. Harness the power of the community to help make government work better

Government, at its core, is about doing together what we can’t do alone.

In Tim O’Reilly’s Government as a Platform, he talks about how the web has inspired a new generation of people to use creativity and collaboration to address challenges facing our country and the world. In order for governments to take advantage of all of the people who want to help, they need to open up and allow for a cross-section of the community to participate, and collaborate on shaping the programs and services that are offered.
