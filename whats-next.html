What's Next

Now that the foundation has been laid for a strong and growing international network , the Code for All community will continue to share our lessons learned, implement programs in partnership with local governments that use technology to improve our communities and document our learnings. We, as Code for All, will act as a platform for anyone, anywhere to start a civic technology project where they live.

And all of our efforts will continue to be better because we are connected.

Building the Civic Technology Infrastructure

Code for All is committed to creating a space for resource sharing and application discoverability to operate at scale and better address shared challenges together. This will take the form of a virtual platform where people can connect to tools and resources from wherever they are, enabling the global civic technology community.

Relationship Building

Face to face meetings strengthen the community and foster the cross-pollination of ideas across the world. International meetings that bring together our stakeholders will be held periodically to continue to strengthen the network and foster meaningful exchange and shared learnings.

Resources

We will continue to document our lessons learned and create actionable resources that are useful to our existing partners and others who are just getting started. Code for America has created a “Fellowship Toolkit” that walks the reader through how we implement our fellowship program and provides useful guidance on thinking through CfA Fellowship Program implementation for one’s local context. Code for the Caribbean has created a useful guide to innovation fellowships. As a community, Code for All members will continue to populate our network platform with lessons learned, toolkits and thoughtful documentation and analysis of our programs.
