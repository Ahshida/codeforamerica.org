Starting in 2015, we are focusing our iterative, user-centered, and data-driven approach to government primarily in three areas: health, economic development, and safety & justice.

Health & Human Services

Health and Dignity by Default

Building technology that puts the user first can make it easier and more affordable to feed, shelter, and treat those in need. Code for America partners with local governments, service organizations, and foundations to promote a culture of health by applying human centered design and modern technology to the delivery of services.

Safety & Justice

Fair, Safe, and Just

Getting the right information to the right person at the right time -- in the right way -- can make our communities safer and more just. Code for America partners with local governments, foundations, and others to make our public safety and criminal justice systems work for the people, by the people, in the 21st century.

Get in touch

  • CourtBot

    Instead of traveling to the courthouse and standing in line — sometimes for hours — the 200,000 Atlanta residents who receive traffic tickets each year can now text their citation number to get information on how to pay the ticket or get reminders about their court dates.

    The Jail Population Management Dashboard helps judges and other stakeholders understand the conditions in the metro jail and use this data to visualize how their decisions affect program, facility and inmate outcomes.

    Where's My School Bus? is a location information app allowing parents to track their child’s bus in real time - reducing the time unsupervised and in harms way.

Economic Development

Economic Opportunity for Everyone

Redesigning how workers, entrepreneurs, and businesses interact with local government can foster the kind of growth a whole community can participate in. Code for America partners with local government to develop tools and approaches for stimulating economic vitality for everyone.

Get in touch

  • OpenCounter

    OpenCounter helps entrepreneurs navigate the business registration process. The app asks the right questions, in the right order, so applicants can see the exact requirements, fees, and processing time relevant to them across city departments.

    Learn More

    • Website
    • GitHub

  • BizFriendly.

    BizFriend.ly is a digital literacy resource where small to mid-sized businesses can build the skills and understanding to get their company online. Lessons provide entrepreneurs with an easy-to-follow, real-time, step-by-step guide to various programs and web-based services.

    It’s one thing to know that your city is spending hundreds of millions of dollars improving infrastructure. It’s another to know that they’re finally going to fix your broken sidewalk.
