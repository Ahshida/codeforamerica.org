We help governments strengthen the safety net.

We partner with governments to build digital services focused on users, and help governments transform themselves into learning organizations along the way.

Our Mission

Code for America’s Focus Areas are where we demonstrate the human impact of 21st Century Government and improved digital services. In 2014, we launched our first three Focus Areas in Health, Economic Development, and Safety and Justice.

Within the Health Focus Area, we use the term health as a community outcome that is deeply affected by a communities lived environment, and the enrollment, usability, and delivery of digital services, especially for the safety net. We take an expansive view of the local agencies and services that contribute to this outcome, but typically work with Health Departments, Human Service Agencies, Public Health Agencies, and the organizations that deliver government funded health services such as public hospitals and clinics, and non profits like the Food Bank.

Finding little wins that you can discover along the way, moments when people can come together and solve some small issue — that’s critical to the experience of innovation.

Our programs

Code for America’s Focus Areas support and integrate our diverse programming so that the work’s impact is greater than the sum of its parts. Our work consists of three primary thruts of work: the Health Lab, Health Fellowships, and Product Residencies.

Health Lab

The Health Focus Area is also experimenting with infrastructure to invest and experiment over time in high potential digital services through the Health Lab. Our initial work in the Health Lab builds on the 2013 Fellowship in San Francisco to improve the enrollment and usability of the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP).

Health Fellowships

The Fellowship Program is an intensive year of deep partnership with one local city or county around a new or improved digital service. The output includes a alpha product for that digital service, an understanding of the problem space in one community informed by our broader Gov Network, a local government partner with greater capacity to demonstrate the Principles for 21st Century Government, and strong relationships across related non-profits and delivery organizations that can be leveraged in the future.
