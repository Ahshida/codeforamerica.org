Health and social services delivered with dignity, by default.

We partner with local governments to build and grow digital health and human services focused on users, and help governments transform themselves along the way.

Our Mission

Code for America’s Focus Areas are where we demonstrate the human impact of 21st Century Government and improved digital services. In 2014, we launched our first three Focus Areas in Health, Economic Development, and Safety and Justice.

Within the Health Focus Area, we use the term health as a community outcome that is deeply affected by a communities lived environment, and the enrollment, usability, and delivery of digital services, especially for the safety net. We take an expansive view of the local agencies and services that contribute to this outcome, but typically work with Health Departments, Human Service Agencies, Public Health Agencies, and the organizations that deliver government funded health services such as public hospitals, clinics, and non profits like food banks.

Focus Area Structure

Code for America’s Focus Areas support and integrate our diverse programming so that the work’s impact is greater than the sum of its parts. Our work consists of two primary components: the Health Lab and Health Fellowships.

Health Lab

The Health Lab experiments and invests over time in high potential digital services. Our initial work in the Health Lab builds on the 2013 Fellowship in San Francisco to improve the enrollment and usability of the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP).

In 2015, the Health Lab is conducting ongoing research and product development to both improve the SNAP participation rate in California and dignify the experience of obtaining and using CalFresh benefits.

You can follow all of the Health Lab's work on Github. You can also track performance against our key product indicators on our public metrics page.

Health Lab Work
  • Balance

    Balance allows people who receive food stamps to check their balance via SMS. Balance brings a little dignity and convenience to the business of managing benefits—a surprisingly difficult (and high stakes) task. Balance also shows governments that user experience improvements are possible with limited cost and integration with existing systems.

  • Clean

    Clean is a simple and dignified online application for CalFresh. With Clean, enrollment assisters can serve more people in less time, and fewer people need expert assistance at all. We believe that best-in-class user experience, product marketing and partnerships that Clean will be able to meaningfully increase the number of people eligible for CalFresh enrolled in the program.

  • Connect

    Connect helps social service clients and enrollment assisters avoid wasting their day on hold. Connect allows users to initiate a call to a social services agency via SMS, and get a call back when a human is on the other line. With Connect, people can use the time they would have spent on hold with family, on work, or any number of more productive and less stressful activities.

  • EBT Near Me

    EBT Near Me helps social service clients find important places they can use their EBT card. On their phone or when first enrolled, people can find ATMs without fees and CalFresh retail locations. Our goal is to reduce the massive sum of benefit dollars lost to bank fees (link), and help people find new shopping options.

Health Fellowships

The Fellowship Program is an intensive year of deep partnership with one local city or county around a new or improved digital service. The output includes a alpha product for that digital service, an understanding of the problem space in one community informed by our broader Gov Network, a local government partner with greater capacity to demonstrate the Principles for 21st Century Government, and strong relationships across related non-profits and delivery organizations that can be leveraged in the future.

Our Partners

Our partners spans the public, non-profit, and private sectors. Our work to improve digital service delivery is built on foundational relationships with state and local government.
