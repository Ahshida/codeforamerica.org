The 2014 Fellowship in Long Beach

In 2014, Code for America partnered with Long Beach to reduce avoidable utilization of emergency resources and improve health outcomes. Their research showed a small percentage of the population in Long Beach disproportionately uses emergency services. In 2013, 52 percent of Long Beach emergency medical calls came from only 10 percent of addresses.

To reduce high frequency or “super-utilization” of emergency medical services, the Fellows built AddressIQ to facilitate high-touch human interventions. By using meta-data to identify locations withs a high concentration of emergency calls, the Fellows' app empowered the city to direct resources to locations that would be helped most.
