Join Us: The Fourth Annual Code for America Summit

September 23 – 25, 2014 | The Yerba Buena Center | San Francisco, CA

The annual Summit is when the Code for America civic innovation community gathers in person for three days of collaborating, connecting, and learning.

It's a roll-up-your-sleeves kind of event that convenes innovators from dozens of local governments and mixes it up with civic-minded technologists, designers, community organizers, and entrepreneurs.

Register today to participate — and join the movement for government that works in the 21st century.

REGISTER NOW

  • Government

    The Summit is one of the few opportunities for civic leaders from all levels of government, from cities and counties big and small, to come together and discuss their vision, opportunities, and challenges.

  • Technologists

    Developers, designers, researchers, product managers, data enthusiasts, and technologists of all varieties join the Summit to share stories, ideas and code. These active citizens are turning passion into impact.

  • Community

    Transformation doesn’t happen without engagement in a larger ecosystem. The startups, entrepreneurs, foundations, civic hackers, and tech companies who sustain our communities also participate in the dialogue.

What We'll Be Up To

Keynotes and panels

Industry leaders and experts in the field (from inside government and out) sharing insights and stories from the front lines.

Fellowship demos

This year’s city partners and fellows will demo their apps and share what they learned during the Fellowship.

Breakouts and workshops

Deep dive sessions on practical topics like civic design, open data standards, lean analytics for government, and improving procurement processes.

Unconference and hackathon

Participant-led discussions on topics you care about and hands-on sessions with civic technologists to work on interesting problems — no coding skills required.

The Civic Tech Fair

Discover and try out cutting-edge technology that's improving public service and citizen experience.

Networking

Lots of time to connect with new friends and old, from receptions to small-group dinners to after-hours Ignite talks.
