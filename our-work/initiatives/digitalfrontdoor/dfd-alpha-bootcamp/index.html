Digital Front Door Alpha Program - A Web Redesign Bootcamp for Government Communicators.

Apply Now

The Website is your City's Digital Front Door

Residents expect to find the same services there they would find inside city hall. Further, we now know that 64% of American adults own a smartphone of some kind and many underserved citizens use a smartphone as their only means of online access. People need and expect to do city business from wherever they are. In this new world, digital first means access for all.

Bootcamp Overview

The selected five city cohort will prepare for a redesign that makes your website inclusive, service oriented, and mobile ready.

Learn redesign best practices, by building an alpha website for a department service. An entire website redesign can be a daunting process, often taking years to complete. An Alpha site (a small but public experiment) allows you to reduce risk, try new ideas and get resident feedback early.

Over 10 weeks, participants will develop skills by prototyping an alpha website that addresses a specific resident need. Finish with a framework and practice ready to apply to a city wide redesign.

Alpha Package:

  • $5000 - FREE for first bootcamp cohort
  • Alpha website that models a new approach to addressing a resident need
  • Weekly workshops and hands-on support from Code for America
  • Access to a network of practitioners
  • A design brief and template RFP

What you commit:

  • Attendance of at least one staff person at weekly online workshops
  • Implemenentation work with alpha department colleauges on weekly milestones
  • Use collaborative tools like Slack and Google Docs (in some cases this may mean you need to do your Bootcamp work from a personal computer)

More about the Redesign Bootcamp

  • Example workshop sessions:

    • Baseline Performance and Develop Analytics
    • Writing for the web
    • Iterative Design & Development
    • Organizing your web team
    • Project scoping, user needs definition
    • User testing and recruiting
    • Design values and briefs
    • Digital first publishing and workflows

  • Example user needs, showing how we’re using DfD alphas to improve service delivery

    • Parking information: “How do I pay a parking ticket?”
    • Parks and Recreation programming: “When is the next lap swim?”
    • Legislative participation: “When is the next council meeting?”
    • Job seekers. “What jobs are offered by city hall”
    • Special events. “How do I plan a public event?
    • Trash pick up. “When is trash pick-up for my street?”
    • Report service requests. “My pothole needs filling!”
